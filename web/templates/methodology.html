Methodology

What the numbers on this site mean, and what they don't.

What GPU Pulse tracks

We publish hourly snapshots of GPU cloud list prices and, where we can observe it, whether capacity appears available. Historical data is kept — we don't overwrite past observations.

How fresh is the data?

Numbers on this site refresh about once per hour (UTC). The timestamp at the top of each page shows when the latest snapshot was computed. Expect up to one hour of lag.

Prices

All prices are shown as USD per GPU-hour so you can compare across providers. We normalize GPU names (e.g. H100-SXM-80GB) because each provider labels hardware differently. Multi-GPU offers store machine $/hr and divide by GPU count.

The index headline is the median eligible on-demand price. “From $X” is the eligible on-demand floor. Spot / interruptible prices never set the headline; when present they appear as a separate “spot from” line.

Marketplace eligibility

Raw marketplace listings are noisy. Every Vast offer is still stored, but only index-eligible offers can set floors and medians:

  • Reported host reliability ≥ 0.95
  • Host marked verified

Ineligible listings still count toward volume stats; they just cannot move the published floor. Catalog and capacity-API providers (SkyPilot, Lambda, RunPod) are treated as eligible for their published rate cards.

Trends

7-day and 24-hour percentage changes track the rolling median of eligible on-demand prices — not the raw minimum. If there are fewer than 5 eligible offers or fewer than 3 providers in the latest sample, we show insufficient data instead of a percentage.

Availability

Not every provider publishes capacity information. Where we have a signal, we show it; where we don't, we label it no signal rather than 0%. Zero would be a claim; unknown is not.

  • High — capacity looked available in most checks over the past 24 hours
  • Med — mixed signals
  • Low — rarely looked available recently
  • No signal — we track list prices but have no availability probe for this GPU

Limitations

  • List prices may exclude reserved discounts, credits, or enterprise agreements.
  • Marketplace and catalog data can lag reality by minutes to hours.
  • Availability indicators are observational, not a guarantee of successful provisioning.
  • Parser upgrades use a version stamp — we don't rewrite past rows; new rollups prefer current parser data.

API

JSON endpoints mirror the data on this site. Access is free and rate-limited. No account required.

  • /api/v1/index
  • /api/v1/prices?gpu=…
  • /api/v1/availability?gpu=…
  • /api/v1/meta
